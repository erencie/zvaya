Grab the Course Contents Faster

By Xiaoni Lai (Olivia) 5 Oct 2013

I seemed to be the most hardworking graduate student in our lab. Joey kept going out to meet all kinds of friends in order to improve his oral English skills. Jack and Adriel were always chatting and made critics on some latest news, such as the shutdown of US government recently. Only me. Studying. Carrying bulky textbooks borrowed from library all the time. But do I really do more studies than them? Not really.

Today I spent one whole day (okay not the whole day as I finished my breakfast at home quite late) in the Dana Porter Library, reading the Computer Networking textbook. Soon I realized that this is the reason why I spent so much time and couldn't catch up the class schedule all the time.

Honestly speaking, reading the textbook is relaxing and fun. The author is always describing things in a very detailed, sometimes humour, way. However, the text tends to be quite wordy in the textbook, completely different from the succinctness seen from a research paper. Because of this, I always start to feel not so concentrated when I read the textbook. This is analogous to the scenario when I go to listen to a talk which is clear, detailed but very wordy and containing too much information; I would always deviate my attention to somewhere else during the course.

A more efficient way is to take out the lecture notes, follow the contents in the lecture notes and look up relevant portions in the textbook whenever I feel needed. As the lecture notes are succinct, I need to pay full attention to figure out what each slide means. Moreover, actively searching a certain topic in the textbook makes me more engaged in the learning process, compared to passively absorbing the vast amount of materials unfiltered in the textbook.

I did so in the last one hour in library and quickly finished up the few algorithms and protocols at the end of that chapter. Still, spent too much time on the Network Layer chapter and haven't started looking at the Link Layer one. Anyway, I found a more efficient way to study this course. That's my gain today.
